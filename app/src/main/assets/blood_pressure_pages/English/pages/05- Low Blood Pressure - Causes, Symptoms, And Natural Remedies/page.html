Low Blood Pressure - Causes, Symptoms, And Natural Remedies

Low blood pressure (known as hypotension) is usually indicated by systolic pressure less than 90 millimetres of mercury (mmHg) or diastolic pressure less than 60 millimetres of mercury (mmHg). The general rule of thumb is only to be concerned with hypotension that prompts symptoms like dizziness, fainting, blurred vision, etc.

If this happens for a long period, it can cause serious consequences, including:

• Shock

• kidney failure

• Stroke

• Heart attack

Or if hypotension is severe, it can be life-threatening.

Causes Of Hypotension

There are several potential causes of low blood pressure or hypotension. The causal condition will need to be treated to bring your blood pressure back to normal in some situations.

Some of the most usual causes of low blood pressure include:

• nutritional deficiencies

• prolonged bed rest

• pregnancy

• medications

• severe infections

• allergic reactions

• fall in blood volume

• heart issues

However, low blood pressure can also indicate normal health if a person is not experiencing any symptoms.

Symptoms Of Low Blood Pressure

Some people with hypotension have no symptoms. In such people, low blood pressure is generally not dangerous or concerning.

Some common symptoms of low blood pressure are:

• Dizziness

• Fainting

• Lightheadedness

• Cold or clammy skin

• Pale skin

• Weak and rapid pulse

• Shock

• Confusion

• Rapid and shallow breathing

However, the appearance of one or two symptoms may signal a problem. Low blood pressure can cause the following:

Orthostatic hypotension :

While it is uncommon, some people may have symptoms of low blood pressure only when standing, and it's often referred to as orthostatic hypotension. It is typically not serious as long as postural changes do not lead to a rapid drop in blood pressure, as it may lead to fainting.

Shock

Low blood pressure can lead to shock in more extreme cases. A state of shock means that a person has reduced blood flow throughout the body, and it could damage their organs at a cellular level or cause death if left untreated !

The symptoms of shock include the following :

• rapid or shallow breathing

• confusion or disorientation

• clammy skin

• rapid heartbeat

• dizziness

• fainting

• weak pulse

• inability to concentrate

• blurred or distorted vision

• nausea

• fatigue

A person experiencing symptoms of severe shock needs crucial medical attention.

Natural remedies for low blood pressure

These are some simple natural remedies for low blood pressure :

• Eat more salt (increase sodium intake)

• Drink more water and stay hydrated

• Don't skip meals • Wear compression stockings

• Avoid Sudden position changes

• Check your blood sugar level regularly

• Avoid alcoholic beverages

Eat more salt (increase sodium intake)

Diet high in salt (sodium) is a simple and natural remedy for low blood pressure. Intaking more salt will be beneficial for you if you have hypotension as sodium in the table salt help increase your blood volume by retaining more fluids in the circulatory system. In this way, the blood pressure rises.

But if you have hypertension more often, you should avoid high salt intake.

Drink more water and stay hydrated

One way to avoid blood pressure problems is to increase the amount of water intake on a regular basis. Drinking a sufficiently high amount of water can help improve your blood volume, which can also help you prevent low blood pressure if provoked by some illness.

Don't skip meals

If you have hypotension more often, you should try not to skip meals as it is essential for you to maintain the energy level of your body to cope with low blood pressure or simply avoid it in the first place.

Wear compression stockings

A compression hose is used to help reduce swelling in the lower extremities. Streaming from sitting up or standing up too quickly can also be supported by wearing a knee-high compression hose. Compression hosiery is also used to help people with varicose veins, which bulge like a water balloon due to sluggish circulation.

Avoid Sudden position changes

Sitting up or standing up suddenly can stress the heart and blood pressure, thus causing dizziness and headaches. In cases like these, a compression hose can help pump blood faster through the body so that there will be less chance of feeling lightheaded or dizzy.

Check your blood sugar level regularly

High blood sugar levels and diabetes may lead to low blood pressure. Diets that are excessively high in carbohydrates can lead to too much urination. To avoid volume depletion, ensure your diet contains enough protein and carbohydrates while avoiding foods with added sugars or fats.

You can monitor this by wearing a home monitor to check your blood sugar levels throughout the day. See your doctor regularly to find the proper diet, exercise plan, and medication to help balance your blood sugar levels.

Avoid alcoholic beverages

Alcohol tends to lower blood pressure further. People with hypotension or frequent low blood pressure should take extra measures to ensure they do not drink undue amounts of alcohol as it may lead to complications such as fainting or giddiness when standing.

Recognize triggers

Learn to recognize triggers and symptoms. If you feel faint or lightheaded, put your head down or lay down to help rectify the effects of low blood pressure. These symptoms pass quickly, but if they persist just after getting up from a long period of lying down - it's essential to seek medical attention.

If you have orthostatic hypotension, you must avoid anything related to your condition because often, these manifestations are triggered by going up or downstairs, writing, standing for long periods, etc.

Takeaway

Blood pressure usually naturally varies throughout the day to help ensure that your entire body (including the brain, lungs, and heart) — is getting enough blood and oxygen. Occasionally, a sudden drop or decrease in blood pressure can occur if you stand up suddenly. Typically, there's no cause for concern if this happens sometimes or during restful periods (for example, during sleep).

In some cases, having low blood pressure may indicate a problem with an organ or structure; lowering blood pressure can also have adverse side effects such as dizziness and headaches due to insufficient circulation of vital nutrients.
